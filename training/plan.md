## Phase Overview

### Phase 1 — Heat Base (Weeks 5–8: Jun 22 – Jul 19)
**Goal:** Consistent aerobic base in summer heat. Establish brick routine. Sleep discipline.
- Running: 35–45 km/week (brick runs included)
- Long run: 14–18 km (heat-adjusted effort)
- Quality: 1 interval session/week (Tue), 1 moderate quality (Thu)
- No heat runs >45 min after 08:00

### Phase 2 — Volume Build (Weeks 9–12: Jul 20 – Aug 16)
**Goal:** Build to 50–58 km/week. Introduce marathon pace work. Extend long run.
- Running: 48–58 km/week
- Long run: 22–28 km, last 5 km at marathon pace (MP) from Week 10
- Quality: 2 sessions/week (intervals Tue + tempo/progression Thu)
- Brick runs 30 min on Mon/Wed

### Phase 3 — Minden Sharpening (Weeks 13–14: Aug 17–28)
**Goal:** Race-ready for 10km. Reduce volume, maintain quality.
- Running: 40–45 km/week (Week 13), 28–30 km (Week 14 taper)
- Focus: 1000m intervals at 4:05–4:12/km, one 5km tempo at race pace
- Long run capped at 18 km in Week 13

### RACE: Volkslauf Minden — Aug 29
**Target:** 41:30–42:00. Strategy: 4:15/km first 2km, settle to 4:10/km, push last 2km.
No taper week — treat as quality workout within a normal training week.

### Phase 4 — Bremen Build (Weeks 15–16: Aug 30 – Sep 12)
**Goal:** Half marathon sharpening. 2-week mini-peak.
- Running: 48–52 km/week (Week 15), 38–42 km (Week 16 taper)
- Key session: 14 km with 10 km at half marathon pace (4:21–4:23/km)
- Long run: 20–22 km in Week 15, 14–16 km in Week 16

### RACE: AOK Halbmarathon Bremen — Sep 13
**Target:** 1:31:30–1:33:00. Strategy: 4:25/km first 5km, 4:22/km km 5–15, push km 15–21.
Run as strong effort, NOT maximum. Köln is 21 days later.

### Phase 5 — Marathon Specific (Weeks 17–18: Sep 14–27)
**Goal:** Recovery from Bremen + 2 key marathon sessions.
- Week 17 (Sep 14–20): Recovery. Run 35–40 km easy, no quality sessions.
- Week 18 (Sep 21–27): 26–28 km long run with 15 km at MP (4:50–5:00/km). One 10 km tempo.
- Total running: 50–55 km in Week 18.

### Phase 6 — Taper (Week 19: Sep 28 – Oct 3)
**Goal:** Arrive at start line fresh, confident, legs loaded.
- Running: 25–28 km total
- Mon: 8 km easy
- Tue: 6 km with 3×1 km at MP
- Wed: 5 km easy
- Thu: 4 km easy
- Fri: 3 km easy shakeout
- Sat/Sun: REST

### RACE: Generali Köln Marathon — Oct 4
**Target:** Sub 3:30 (3:25 aspirational)
**Pacing strategy (non-negotiable after Berlin 2025):**
- Km 0–10: 5:05/km — feels too easy, that's correct
- Km 10–25: 5:00/km — settle in, read the body
- Km 25–30: 4:55/km if feeling strong
- Km 30+: 4:50/km if feeling strong → finish ~3:22
- If struggling at km 25: hold 5:00 → still finishes ~3:31, adjust in last km
- Never go faster than 4:55/km before km 25 regardless of how good you feel
